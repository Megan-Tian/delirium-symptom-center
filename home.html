Delirium.

An underdiagnosed condition with no universal cause that affects 15 to 50 percent of hospital patients, with especially high prevalance among the elderly population.

Though delirium is a relatively common condition, it is highly misunderstood, with risk factors and triggers ranging from advanced age and dementia to surgery and medication. While elderly patients are significantly more likely to experience delirium, people of all ages are affected.

Enter the Delirium Symptom Center:

A place to educate the public and caregivers about this condition. Featuring a resource guide to help you spot the signs of delirium, and a custom platform for caregivers to track symptoms and optimize care for patients with delirium.

Get started by making an account for an affected patient, and learning more about this condition.
